NEW YORK CITY

Born in Staten Island and raised in Queens, New York - I was shaped by the culture around me. Timberlands, G Unit sneakers, Rocawear, and early 2000s hip hop and R&B were more than trends, they felt like something I belonged to, even as a kid.

That sense of belonging turned into curiosity. I got hooked on pop culture, technology, and science, once even writing NASA to ask if aliens were real. That same curiosity stuck with me, and thankfully hasn't killed me yet.

FORT LAUDERDALE

In 2004, I moved to Florida with my mom, and suddenly the world felt bigger than East Coast winters. Life looked different under palm trees, even with moments like being in the eye of Hurricane Katrina.

Through it all, my mom made sure I still got to just be a kid. Weekends meant Ocean Drive, South Beach, and watching cars roll past the Versace Mansion. That time stays with me not just for where we were, but for what it taught me about enjoying life, even in the middle of everything else.

NYC TO TORONTO

After a quick return to New York from Florida, life hit reset again, this time in Toronto, where my grandmother and great aunts lived. My mom was ready for something new for us. I was 14, so I just went along for the ride.

That move ended up shaping everything. Toronto is where I grew up for real, built lifelong friendships, and became who I am today. Like New York and Florida, it carries its own rhythm, driven by cultures from everywhere. From Caribana to some of the best food in the world, the city taught me how powerful culture can be when it’s shared.

What's Next

Right now it starts with bringing everything together, the places I’ve lived, the people I’ve met, the energy they gave me. I want to turn that into something visual, something people can feel.

Through documentary work, I found a love for telling real stories, not just mine, but others too. That’s something I want to keep building on, across whatever medium lets those stories live the way they deserve.

WORK EXPERIENCE

WEST HARBOUR HEROES - Season 1
STORY PRODUCER

NOV 2025 TO PRESENT

West Harbour Heroes took everything we did in Newfoundland's East Harbour Heroes and brought it to the West Coast. For our first shoot, it allowed to travel to Vancouver Island to shoot with the 442 Search and Rescue team. We filmed in helicopters, parachuted from Twin Otter aircraft, and explored the new Airbus Kingfisher 295 aircraft.

This first adrenaline-filled first episode set the tone for the rest of the season and its nine episodes.

I remotely produced most of the series, researching, building relationships with cast, and working closely with Story Editors and post to shape clear, engaging episodes.

OYSTER ISLAND FEATURE-DOCUMENTARY
DIRECTOR

APRIL 2025 TO PRESENT

My first Feature-Documentary and Director credit, this feature took me to Prince Edward Island, Canada, where life revolves around agriculture and oysters.

Balancing this with West Harbour Heroes pushed me to shift between fast-paced storytelling and a more patient, observational style of filmmaking.

As filming unfolded, the story turned to a disease devastating the oyster population, affecting livelihoods and families. What began as a local issue grew into something much bigger.

The film became a story of community, resilience, and loss. Filmed over a year and a half, it is set to premiere at festivals in 2026.

EAST HARBOUR HEROES - SEASON 3
STORY PRODUCER

JUL 2024 TO NOV 2025

Season three took me across Newfoundland and Labrador, Canada, where I worked closely with cast while traversing both Field Director and Producer roles.

CHASE PRODUCER

OCT 2016 TO MAY 2018

Daily Planet was my entry into the industry in 2016. I started as an intern, moved into research, and eventually became a Chase Producer.

As I grew with the team, I learned every part of the process in a fast paced, live to air environment where stories had to turn around quickly and stay topical.
